Construction Safety Project

It is a well known fact, how hazardous and unsafe the construction industry is, with a construction worker losing their life every 96 minutes. As the second most fatal occu- pation, one naturally questions what underlying factors drive such a high fatality rate. According to key findings from the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), falls, slips, and trips are the leading causes of fatalities in construction. Over the past decade, much has been done in terms of hazard identification and safety inspections. However, one essential el- ement that cannot be overlooked is the feedback from the workers themselves. Studies revolving workers often emphasize the need for more visual aids and more engaging con- tent in safety training sessions. Studies consistently show that an effective safety record is closely tied to training that motivates workers- overcoming the limitations of passive learning, and counters distractions that reduce retention. Additionally, it is important to consider the diversity of the workforce. A significant portion of construction workers are foreign-born, many of whom have limited proficiency in English. For them, visual learning plays a critical role in understanding and retaining safety practices. This paper introduces a novel Augmented Reality (AR) application, created to address these needs by offering continuous training and hazard detection. The AR application projects the leading cause of fatal hazards such as falls, trips, falling objects, as well as electrocution — into real- world construction environments, allowing workers to engage in dangerous situations in a controlled setting. To evaluate the impact of AR-based training, a comparative survey was conducted, that compares the AR training with traditional safety training measures partic- ularly reading and video-based learning methods. The survey focused on two key metrics - knowledge retention and hazard proximity awareness. The results clearly demonstrate that the AR application significantly improves workers’ ability to recognize and respond to the most common fatal hazards, ultimately enhancing their overall hazard awareness and preparedness.
